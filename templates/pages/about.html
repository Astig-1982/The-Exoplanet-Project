"The Earth is a very small stage in a vast cosmic arena."

Carl Sagan

Exoplanets are planets that orbit a star other than our sun. The prefix exo comes from the Greek and means outside; these worlds are far, far outside our own solar system. Astronomers have confirmed more than 4,000 exoplanets orbiting distant stars, with at least 1,000 more awaiting confirmation.

Welcome to OTHER WORLDS - a site that offers you a way to keep track of your favourite exoplanets discovered up to date. You will have your own list of Favourites in which you can add any exoplanet that you wish as soon as it is discovered, by simply entering its details in the form provided. To kick things off, you will also be provided with a Main List containing some of the most relevant exoplanets discovered up to date, from which you can add to your Favourites list.

Contrary to what's depicting in most SCI FI movies, a fun fact about going to other celestial bodies is that your weight differs from that of on Earth due to their different gravity attraction. This site also gives you the opportunity to quickly find out your weight on each of your favourite exoplanet. Enjoy your trip to OTHER WORLDS!
